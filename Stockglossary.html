Stock Market Glossary

  1. Stock: A share in the ownership of a company that entitles the holder to a portion of its assets and earnings.
  2. Stock Market: A public market where stocks and other securities are bought and sold.
  3. Index: A measurement of the value of a section of the stock market, often used as a benchmark to compare the performance of individual stocks or portfolios.
  4. Bull Market: A market in which stock prices are rising and investor confidence is high.
  5. Bear Market: A market in which stock prices are falling and investor confidence is low.
  6. Blue Chip Stock: A stock in a well-established and financially sound company, often considered a safe investment.
  7. Dividend: A portion of a company's earnings that is paid out to shareholders, usually in the form of cash.
  8. Market Capitalization: The total value of a company's outstanding shares, calculated by multiplying the number of shares by the current market price.
  9. P/E Ratio (Price-to-Earnings Ratio): The ratio of a company's stock price to its earnings per share.
  10. Initial Public Offering (IPO): The first time a company offers its stock to the public, often to raise capital for growth or expansion.
  11. Stock Split: A corporate action in which a company divides its existing shares into multiple shares, often to make the stock more affordable and increase liquidity.
  12. Stock Buyback: A corporate action in which a company buys back its own shares from the market, often to reduce the number of outstanding shares and increase the value of remaining shares.
  13. Stock Option: A contract that gives the holder the right, but not the obligation, to buy or sell a specific number of shares of a company's stock at a specific price and time.
  14. Stock Broker: A licensed professional who buys and sells stocks and other securities on behalf of clients.
  15. Margin: A type of loan that investors can use to buy stocks or other securities, using the securities as collateral.
  16. Short Selling: A trading strategy in which an investor borrows shares of a stock and sells them, hoping to buy them back at a lower price to make a profit.
  17. Volatility: A measure of the degree of variation in the price of a stock or other security, often used to assess risk.
  18. Market Order: An order to buy or sell a security at the best available price at the time the order is placed.
  19. Limit Order: An order to buy or sell a security at a specific price or better.
  20. Resistance Level: A price level at which a stock's price tends to encounter selling pressure and struggles to rise further.
  21. Support Level: A price level at which a stock's price tends to encounter buying pressure and struggles to fall further.
